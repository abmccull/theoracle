# The Oracle Art Style Bible

## Core Direction

`The Oracle` should feel like a **sun-drenched, solemn, Delphi-inspired sacred sanctuary** in the ancient Greek world.

The tone is:

- reverent
- mysterious
- elegant
- historical rather than generic fantasy
- intimate in human scale, but grand in spiritual and political weight

## Visual Anchors

- Delphi-like terraces cut into a warm hillside
- weathered marble and limestone
- bronze ritual hardware and ornaments
- terracotta roofs and sun-faded fabric
- laurel leaves, incense smoke, sacred water, carved stone, votive clutter
- parchment-and-bronze UI motifs

## What To Avoid

- medieval Christian clergy silhouettes
- bishop mitres, crosses, cathedral styling, or church interiors
- Roman-imperial monumentality unless intentionally late-game
- glossy mobile-game candy colors
- chibi or toy-like proportions
- generic fantasy MMO armor
- modern SaaS icon language or neon UI

## Character Notes

- priesthood should read as Hellenic ritual staff, not church clergy
- fabrics should feel draped, layered, and practical
- jewelry and metalwork should be bronze/gold and period-inspired
- faces should feel intelligent, weathered, and human rather than hyper-glamorous

## Environment Notes

- terrain should feel dry, stepped, and sacred
- marble is never pristine everywhere; add wear, dust, and sun-bleaching
- water appears only where sacred springs, basins, or ritual pools justify it
- clutter should feel devotional and lived-in, not random or noisy

## Era Variation Rules

- `archaic` assets should feel simpler, heavier, and more local: rougher limestone, less ornament, smaller sanctuaries
- `classical` assets should feel more balanced and refined: cleaner colonnades, better stonework, more deliberate civic-sacred order
- `hellenistic` assets should feel richer and more theatrical: broader stairs, bolder monumentality, more bronze and decorative flourishes
- `romanized` assets should be used sparingly and late: more engineering polish, larger masonry blocks, added infrastructure, but still anchored to the sanctuary's Greek identity
- era changes should read as gradual precinct evolution, not hard world-swap reskins
- every era family must preserve gameplay readability, footprint clarity, and the same fixed isometric camera language

## Asset Priority Standard

When in doubt, optimize for:

1. readable silhouette in isometric gameplay
2. historical/Hellenic mood
3. extraction into sprite or pixel-art cleanup lanes
4. family consistency across variants
